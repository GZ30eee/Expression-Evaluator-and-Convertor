Understanding Infix, Postfix, and Prefix Expressions

Infix expressions are the standard mathematical notations that we use in our daily lives. In an infix expression, operators are written between the operands. For instance, in the expression "2 + 3", "+" is the operator, and "2" and "3" are the operands.

Postfix expressions, also known as Reverse Polish Notation (RPN), represent mathematical expressions with operators placed after their operands. The equivalent postfix expression of "2 + 3" is "2 3 +".

Prefix expressions, or Polish Notation, involve placing operators before operands. In the prefix expression "+ 2 3", "+" is the operator, and "2" and "3" are the operands.

Converting Between Infix, Postfix, and Prefix Expressions

Converting between these notations requires understanding operator precedence and associativity. The Shunting Yard algorithm is a popular method for converting infix expressions to postfix or prefix forms, and similar algorithms exist for conversions in the opposite direction.

Example

Consider the infix expression "2 * 3 + 4".

The equivalent postfix expression is "2 3 * 4 +".

The equivalent prefix expression is "+ * 2 3 4".
